Nim

Nim is a mathematical game of strategy in which two players take turns removing balls from a pile.

On each turn, a player must remove at least one ball, and may remove any number of balls provided it's less than or equal to the set amount of "max".

The goal of the game is to avoid taking the last ball.

On the setup screen you can choose to play against another player, or against the computer. Set the "status" of each player by clicking the "Controlled by" button.

You take balls from the game by clicking any ball on the screen.

The computer is NOT the smartest in the world, but be careful, he will try to force you into taking the last ball.

Have fun playing this fun little game!
